        <!-- What Is Visa Cancellation -->
        <h2>What Is Visa Cancellation?</h2>
        <p>Visa cancellation is the official legal process that terminates your residency visa or entry permit in the UAE immigration system. Your sponsor, an employer, a family member, or a company, files a cancellation request with the appropriate immigration authority.</p>
        <p>Once processed and approved, your visa status changes from "Active" to "Cancelled" or "Closed" in the government database. At that point:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Your legal right to reside in the UAE under that specific visa ends</li>
            <li>A grace period of 30 days begins, during which you may stay legally</li>
            <li>You become eligible to apply for a new visa under a different sponsor</li>
            <li>Your Emirates ID becomes invalid for most official transactions</li>
        </ul>
        <p>Visa cancellation applies to employment visas, family sponsorship visas, investor visas, and domestic worker visas.</p>

        <!-- Why Must Check -->
        <h2>Why You Must Check Your Cancellation Status Personally</h2>
        <p>Sponsors sometimes delay cancellation requests. Others submit requests incorrectly. A few fail to submit them at all. The immigration system does not automatically cancel a visa when your job ends or when your visa expires.</p>
        <p>If your visa remains active after you have left your sponsor:</p>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>New employment blocked:</strong> The immigration system prevents any new sponsor from processing a work visa</li>
            <li><strong>Overstay fines accumulate:</strong> Daily fines of AED 50 begin if you remain past your grace period</li>
            <li><strong>Absconding status:</strong> Your sponsor may report you as absconding, causing a travel ban</li>
            <li><strong>Bank account issues:</strong> Banks linked to your Emirates ID may freeze accounts</li>
            <li><strong>Future travel bans:</strong> An unresolved active visa can result in an entry ban</li>
        </ul>
        <p>Checking your status directly in the government system is the only way to confirm cancellation.</p>

        <!-- Method 1 -->
        <h2>Method 1: Check Cancellation Status by Passport Number (ICP System)</h2>
        <p>Use this method for visas issued in Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, Ajman, Umm Al Quwain, Ras Al Khaimah, or Fujairah. The Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs & Port Security (ICP) manages immigration for these six emirates.</p>
        <div class="step-box">
            <p><strong>Step-by-step instructions:</strong></p>
            <ol>
                <li>Open your browser and go to the ICP Smart Services website</li>
                <li>From the main menu, click "Public Services."</li>
                <li>Select "File Validity" from the list of services</li>
                <li>On the File Validity page, choose "Passport Information" as your search method</li>
                <li>Select either "Residency" or "Visa" as the document type</li>
                <li>Enter your passport number exactly as it appears (no spaces, dashes, or special characters)</li>
                <li>Enter your passport expiry date in the format requested (typically DD/MM/YYYY)</li>
                <li>Select your nationality from the dropdown menu</li>
                <li>Complete the captcha verification</li>
                <li>Click "Submit"</li>
            </ol>
        </div>
        <p>Within 5 to 10 seconds, the system displays your current visa status. If cancelled, the status field shows "Cancelled" or "Closed" along with the official cancellation date.</p>
        <p><strong>What the result screen shows:</strong> Current visa status (Active, Cancelled, Expired, Under Process), Visa expiry date (if active), Cancellation date (if cancelled), Your UID number, Your file number. Take a screenshot of this screen or save it as a PDF as proof of cancellation.</p>

        <!-- Method 2 -->
        <h2>Method 2: Check Cancellation Status by Passport Number (GDRFA Dubai System)</h2>
        <p>Dubai operates its own immigration system through the General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA). The GDRFA website does not support passport number searches. You must use the mobile app.</p>
        <div class="step-box">
            <p><strong>Step-by-step instructions:</strong></p>
            <ol>
                <li>Download the GDRFA Dubai App from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store</li>
                <li>Open the app and tap the Services icon (grid or menu icon in the bottom navigation bar)</li>
                <li>Scroll down to the "Entry Permit Services" section</li>
                <li>Tap "File Status Inquiry"</li>
                <li>Select "Passport Information" as your search parameter</li>
                <li>Select your visa type from the dropdown menu</li>
                <li>Enter your passport number, passport expiry date, and nationality</li>
                <li>Submit the inquiry</li>
            </ol>
        </div>
        <p>The app returns your current visa status. A cancelled Dubai visa shows as "cancelled" with a date stamp.</p>

        <!-- Troubleshooting -->
        <h2>Troubleshooting Common System Errors</h2>
        <p><strong>Error: "No Record Found" repeatedly</strong><br>Solutions: Check for typos (number "0" vs letter "O"), verify correct portal (ICP for six emirates, GDRFA for Dubai), try different search method (UID instead of passport), wait 24-48 hours for new submissions, visit the government typing center (10-30 AED) for a printed report.</p>
        <p><strong>Error: Portal times out</strong><br>Both portals have 60-second inactivity timeouts. Prepare information before opening the portal.</p>
        <p><strong>Error: Different results from different search methods</strong><br>This indicates a data mismatch, most commonly duplicate UID records. Visit an immigration center in person.</p>
        <div class="info-card">
            <strong>The UID Merge Problem:</strong><br>
            The system sometimes contains two UID numbers for the same person. Causes include passport renewal without proper linking, having a visit visa before residency, or typing errors.<br>
            Signs: "No Record Found" despite a valid visa, different results from different search methods, Emirates ID not matching the visa record.<br>
            Fix: Visit GDRFA Al Jaffliya (Dubai) or ICP Customer Happiness Center (other emirates). Bring old and new passports. Officer merges records in approximately 20 minutes.
        </div>
